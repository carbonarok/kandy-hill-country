The shape of it

Five days, one base, no rushing.

We settle into Kandy for the whole trip and let the island come to us — the Sacred Tooth at dusk, a dawn run at the Lion Rock, hidden 14th-century temples, and a slow drive west to the coast to finish. One van, one driver, zero logistics headaches.

Tea plantations and elephants are deliberately left off — we're doing those elsewhere on the trip.

Getting there

Down from Ella, into the hills.

We arrive from Ella on 2 November. The famous blue train through the tea country is one of the great rail journeys on earth — if it's running, we'll ride it.

⚠ Read first — the train

The Ella–Kandy hill line was badly damaged by the late-2025 floods & landslides and was still suspended into early 2026, with full reopening targeted around mid-2026. Whether the 2 Nov service runs isn't certain yet.

  • Check railway.gov.lk about 4–6 weeks out.
  • Reserved seats open exactly 30 days before — and sell out fast.
  • Even running, the scenic ride is ~7 hrs, so Day 1 is an arrival-evening either way.

Best move for a group: a private van & driver (~5–6 hrs) for the whole stay. It absorbs the train risk and makes the Sigiriya day trip and the Negombo transfer painless.

Arrival & Kandy by night

An easy first evening after the journey.

  • Kandyan cultural dance — drumming, costumes, a fire-walking finale

Kandy's heritage heart

The city's culture, unhurried.

  • Temple of the Tooth at the morning offering ceremony, plus its museum
  • Old Kandy on foot — National Museum, the art & culture centre, the market
  • Udawatta Kele — rainforest reserve right behind the temple
  • A relaxed evening before the early Sigiriya start

03Wed 4 Nov

Sigiriya day trip★

A dawn start north to the Lion Rock; Dambulla's caves on the way home.

  • Sigiriya Lion Rock (UNESCO) — frescoes, mirror wall, the lion's paws, the summit palace
  • Dambulla Cave Temple — five caves of Buddha statues & ceiling murals
  • The early start is essential — climb in the cool, beat the crowds and the haze

Good to know

A few small things that make it smooth.

One van, one driver

Hired for the whole stay — it makes Sigiriya and the temple loops effortless, and there's no daily haggling.

Early starts pay off

Especially Sigiriya. We climb in the cool of the morning, well ahead of the crowds and the midday haze.

Temple dress code

Shoulders & knees covered, shoes off. No shorts at Dambulla — slip-ons make the in-and-out easy.

Carry cash

Several sites are cash-only and fees are small — Sigiriya is the exception at roughly US$30 a head.

Sun & water

Hat, sunscreen, plenty of water. The climbs are exposed and warm — the heat sneaks up on you.

November weather

Warm days with afternoon showers, so outdoor plans go in the mornings — which suits the early starts anyway.
